Below is a collection of projects that I have either completed as part of my university coursework or have pursued while involved in various research groups.

Research Involvement

The University of Tokyo {Feb '13 - Sep '14}

Advisor: Yoshinori Tamada

Skills used: C, OpenMP on many-core architecture (Xeon Phi)

Conducted research on how to make the estimation of large scale Bayesian networks parallely scalable. Most Bayesian network applications, like decision networks, rely only on the estimation of a few tens of variables. Some applications, frequently in computational biology, require estimation for networks with thousands of variables. I made an effort to study how to tradeoff accuracy while speeding up modern computational methods enough to allow estimation of networks for applications with large scale needs.

Related links: Tamada's Gene Network Estimation Project

The University of Tokyo {Apr '12 - Feb '13}

Advisor: Reiji Suda

Skills used: C++, Convex Optimization Packages (Semidefinite Programming)

Original Proposal

I conducted brief research into increasing the computing scale of multiple kernel learning in Support Vector Machine (SVM) implementations. I became interested in SVM learning after studying character recognition applications in some independent study projects and wanted to continue formal study in a graduate course.

Related links (not my works): Parallel Semidefinite Programming Solver, SHOGUN toolbox

Purdue University {Aug '10 - July '11}

Advisor: Zhilan Feng

Project Description (NSF Link)

Feng, Zhilan, et al. "Plant toxins and trophic cascades alter fire regime and succession on a boreal forest landscape." Ecological Modelling 244 (2012): 79-92.

Skills used: C/C++, R, Octave, Mathematica

This research focuses on the use of mathematical models to study questions concerning Alaska's Boreal ecosystem with the goal of addressing issues of interest to resource management. My role in this project involved integrating a predation model developed by Professor Feng into the landscape modeling software ALFRESCO and matching simulation output to empirical research findings. This was a multidisciplinary joint project between Purdue University and the University of Alaska Fairbanks involving a team of mathematicians, ecologists, statisticians and computer scientists.

NCKU/ Delta Electronics (Tainan, Taiwan) {May '10 - Aug '10}

Advisor: Pau-Choo Chung

Skills used: Embedded C (ARM board), Visual C++ (data visualization on desktop PC)

Professor Chung's lab was working with engineers at Delta Electronics to develop a handheld medical device utilizing photon scattering in skin tissue as a tool for detecting tumors. I worked on an algorithm to determine tissue parameters (volume fraction of water, fat, etc.) based on reflection of light in multilayer tissue at specific wavelengths. This was done through the application of the Nelder-Mead simplex algorithm (implemented in C) and least squares fitting to fit the skin parameters to spectral data.

Tohoku University (Sendai, Japan) {Sep '08 - Aug '09}

Advisor: Ayumi Shinohara

Skills used: C++, OpenCV (computer vision library for C++)

I independently studied data mining methods and participated in weekly laboratory meetings. During my second semester, I began work on a face detection algorithm using the C language openCV library and haar classifiers (Abstract).
